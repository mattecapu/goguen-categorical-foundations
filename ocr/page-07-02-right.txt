Systems S,S' € D(0) are equivalent if they

Consider the hypothesis that some
observed behavior (say example 2, i.e.
Fig.7) came from a finite state machine
(say example 3, Fig.8). Technically, the
hypothesis is that the behaviors are iso-
morphic, i.e. the systems equivalent. The
inclusion of the diagram of Fig.7 in that
of Fig.8b induces a morphism of their
limits (the limit of Fig.8b is just B
again), in the opposite direction. The
hypothesis is that this is an isomorphism.

Another application is in showing that a
system whose objects have some property
also has that property. For example, is a
system of linear objects linear? The
method is to show the (sub-) category of
objects with that property is complete
(finite completeness may be appropriate
for examples such as 3); then the behavior
is in the category. It suffices to check
products and equalizers, which is often
easy. A generalization of this application
is discussed in more detail in Section 7.
